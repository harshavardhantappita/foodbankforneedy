Function of our Food bank

Food banks are nonprofit organizations that collect, store, and distribute food to individuals and families in need. Their primary function is to alleviate hunger and food insecurity within communities. Here's a detailed overview of their functions and how they collect food

Functions of Food Banks:
Food Collection: Food banks gather donations of non-perishable food items, fresh produce, and sometimes prepared meals from various sources such as individuals, businesses, farms, and food manufacturers.Once collected, food is stored in warehouses or distribution centers. These facilities are equipped to handle large quantities of food and ensure proper storage conditions to maintain quality and safety.Food banks distribute food to local agencies and organizations that directly serve people in need, such as soup kitchens, shelters, food pantries, and community centers. These agencies then distribute the food to individuals and families facing food insecurity.
